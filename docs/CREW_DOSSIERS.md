# CREW DOSSIERS — the twelve who might sign on

Every one of these people exists in the sector before you meet them and keeps
existing after. Each has a **role ability** (the mechanical reason to hire),
a **want** (the personal quest the trust system matures), a **wound** and a
**tell** (how the hidden sheet leaks), a **secret** (sometimes a landmine),
and an **agenda** — the honest or dishonest thing they are actually doing with
their berth on your ship. Two per role. Found in cantinas on their home worlds
(data: `src/content/characters.json`), except where noted.

Legend: ★ honest · ☆ conflicted · ✖ dishonest

---

## PILOTS — the ship dodges what they see coming

### ★☆ Dessa "Dez" Okonkwo — *Kestrel's Rest / Port Solace*
**Ability:** pilot — −15% fuel burn, dodges meteor swarms, +25% flee/run odds.
**Backstory:** Third-generation wheat farmer who learned to fly in a crop-duster
older than the Union's claim to the Rest. The farm went to the bank the same
season the Union revised agricultural tariffs; she went to the black. Flies low
over any farmland on approach — every time — just to look.
**Intentions:** Honest about everything except the mail. Wants the farm back,
credit by credit.
**Alliances:** Frontier Compact — her sister organizes grain co-ops alongside
people like Organizer Yun. Dez couriers Compact mail through your registry:
never contraband, just words the Union would rather not travel. If a compliance
scan ever goes deep, "just words" will be your problem to explain.
**Influence on the player:** The soft on-ramp to Frontier politics. Keeps you
honest about food stores (she counts them twice — she remembers hunger).

### ✖ Callum Vex — *Haven's Folly*
**Ability:** pilot — same numbers as Dez, flashier landings.
**Backstory:** Circuit racer on the Folly's illegal ring courses until he bet
his own purse against a Red Sky book and lost both. Flies like the debt is
behind him, because it is.
**Intentions:** Dishonest by necessity. Every port call, part of his pay goes
down a hole. He's wanted under another name in three systems.
**Alliances:** Red Sky Syndicate — as *collateral*, not member. Someday the
hole stops asking for money and asks for your route instead. What he says then
depends entirely on what the ship has meant to him by that day.
**Influence on the player:** The best stick-and-rudder in the sector at the
price of a fuse you can't see. Loyalty is buyable here — the question is
whether you or the Syndicate pays more, and in what currency.

## MECHANICS — hull heals in flight, breakdowns become footnotes

### ★☆ Brix Halloway — *Foundry*
**Ability:** mechanic — repairs hull in flight, jury-rigs damaged modules.
**Backstory:** Foundry yard prodigy, washed out for "unauthorized process
improvement" (she fixed a foreman's beloved, broken procedure). Walked out
with a prototype calibration kit that isn't technically hers. The yard boss
hasn't forgotten. Neither has the kit's inventory tag.
**Intentions:** Honest-hearted, technically a thief. Wants to build something
the yards will have to put her name on.
**Alliances:** None — which on Foundry is its own kind of exposure.
**Influence on the player:** Dry-dock bills drop to almost nothing. Docking at
Foundry with her aboard is a story waiting to happen.

### ★ Tomas — *any station undercity (special recruitment)*
**Ability:** mechanic — and the cheapest salary aboard, for a dark reason or a
good one.
**Backstory:** Ledger-bonded to the recyclers, working off a debt engineered
never to shrink. "I'm furniture that fixes things." He is, in fact, the best
listener-to-machines you will ever ship with.
**Recruitment:** Not in any cantina. Find him in an undercity and settle his
bond (400cr): **free him** and he signs on because he chooses to — or **buy
the bond and keep it**, and own a man who will fix your engines beautifully
and never once look you in the eye. The game remembers which captain you were.
**Alliances:** None left. That's what bonding does.
**Influence on the player:** The clearest mirror in the roster. His salary is
the price tag on your mercy.

## GUNNERS — weapons hit 50% harder

### ★ Sgt. Adaeze "Ada" Nnamdi — *Meridian Prime / Port Solace*
**Ability:** gunner — +50% combat damage (+65% once bonded through her quest).
**Backstory:** Union marine gunnery sergeant. Eleven years ago she was in
orbit over Meridian when sixty thousand people died in a "reactor accident"
she watched happen through a targeting scope. She deserted three weeks later,
which the Union files under treason and she files under the only order she's
proud of.
**Intentions:** Honest to the bone. Wants to bury someone properly, back where
they're from.
**Alliances:** None on paper; the Survivors' Tribunal would call her a witness
if it ever learns she exists. (See THE RECKONING.)
**Landmine:** `union_deserter` — a Union compliance scan can take her off your
ship in cuffs, mid-game, for good. Flying her through Union space is a choice
you make every single day, and the crew knows it.
**Influence on the player:** The Meridian massacre stops being backstory and
starts standing in your galley. The single most campaign-charged hire.

### ☆ Rook Vandermeer — *Verge Station / Haven's Folly*
**Ability:** gunner — same guns, better war stories.
**Backstory:** Flew under a black flag out of the Folly. Left the life on
good-enough terms that his old crew still thinks he's casing ships for them.
He isn't. Probably. Scans every new dock for a face he never finds — you'll
notice it before he tells you why.
**Intentions:** Lapsed-dishonest: wants to stop running long enough to sleep
through a night. Knows the pirate crews *by name* — where they drink, what
they fly, who's holding a grudge.
**Alliances:** Red Sky (expired, disputed). Pirates of the Kestrel lanes
(deniable).
**Influence on the player:** Combat knowledge with a past attached. When
pirates hail you, some of them know him. That cuts both ways, forever.

## MEDICS — sick passengers pay full fare, medical contracts unlock

### ★ Dr. Imogen Hale — *Meridian Prime / Foundry*
**Ability:** medic — cures sick passengers, unlocks serum contracts.
**Backstory:** Union research directorate, two doors down from the
dispersal-modeling teams. She didn't know what the models were for until she
did; she has been practicing frontier medicine like a debt ever since. She is
also dying, and has told exactly no one, and does her own stitches.
**Intentions:** Honest. Atonement, one patient at a time, on a deadline only
she knows.
**Alliances:** None — but her old badge would open doors in the Reckoning, and
her old colleagues include the kind of men who seek immunity deals.
**Influence on the player:** The conscience hire. Her quest is a clock; helping
her costs you nothing but timing, and not helping her is something the crew
will remember you didn't do.

### ✖ "Saint" Corbin — *Haven's Folly / Verge Station*
**Ability:** medic — the best hands on the frontier, no license, no questions.
**Backstory:** Under-deck street-doc who has patched Kord's collectors,
Syndicate knife wounds, and anyone else who could pay in supplies. Beloved
down there. "Saint" is only half a joke.
**Intentions:** Dishonest in retail quantities: he trades patient talk and
ship gossip to an information broker (ask Wren, cantina-side) to keep his
clinic stocked. Nothing he hears aboard is safe, including your manifests.
**Alliances:** Wren's information market; the undercity generally.
**Influence on the player:** Cheap, brilliant, leaky. The hire that teaches
you the difference between a good man and a safe one.

## COOKS — rations stretch 25% further

### ★ Bapu Okafor — *Kestrel's Rest / Port Solace*
**Ability:** cook — crew eats 25% less, morale eats better than that.
**Backstory:** Born on a generation ship that never made port, raised in the
pilgrim flotillas after the ship's drive finally, politely died. Grandfather
to every crew he feeds. Hums while he cooks — an old flotilla hymn, four
seconds long, that you will eventually recognize from somewhere much worse.
**Intentions:** Honest as bread. Wants passage, eventually, to Elysium — if
it's real. (It is. See the main arc.)
**Alliances:** The pilgrim flotillas; friendly with the Church of the Open
Door without believing a word of it — he thinks they heard the same hymn and
got the words wrong.
**Influence on the player:** The Long Silence's gentlest foreshadowing, three
meals a day. When the Broadcast comes, watch him.

### ☆✖ Nyla Sorrensen — *Verge Station*
**Ability:** cook — stretches rations; audits the pantry like a crime scene.
**Backstory:** Quartermastered a mining outfit at Teller's Claim. Left with
the ledger's rounding errors two weeks before the Claim went quiet — which
makes her either lucky, or the only person who ever profited from the
Silence. She doesn't talk about it sober.
**Intentions:** Petty-dishonest: skims your galley budget out of pure habit.
Fiercely, violently loyal once she decides you're crew. Wanted under another
name in three systems, none of which she'll name.
**Alliances:** None living. Survivor's guilt with a savings account.
**Influence on the player:** A Silence survivor in your galley — when worlds
start going dark she has *opinions*, and nightmares, and possibly information.

## QUARTERMASTERS — contracts pay 15% more

### ★ Elias Wren-Kohl — *Port Solace*
**Ability:** quartermaster — +15% contract pay (+22% bonded), cheaper bribes
he pretends not to notice arranging.
**Backstory:** Union customs auditor who tried to audit Harbormaster Vance.
The audit was flawless; his career was over in six weeks — reassigned,
un-personed, pensioned into a chair on the station he tried to clean. Keeps
your books immaculate. Keeps a second, smaller book about her.
**Intentions:** Honest to a fault — literally; the fault is Vance.
**Alliances:** Union-lean by training, nobody's by experience.
**Influence on the player:** Every credit accounted for, and a standing grudge
with the most connected fixer on the docks. Hiring him is a quiet declaration
at the Harbormaster's window.

### ✖ Mirelle "Miri" Datta — *Haven's Folly*
**Ability:** quartermaster — the same +15% that Elias earns honestly, plus
margins that improve in ways you shouldn't ask about.
**Backstory:** Red Sky bookkeeper "on sabbatical." The Syndicate doesn't have
employees so much as investments, and Miri is one of their better ones.
**Intentions:** Dishonest with a smile: copies of your manifests improve
somebody else's picture of the shipping lanes. The Syndicate grooms reliable
captains the way banks groom good borrowers. You're being groomed. The pay
really is better, though.
**Alliances:** Red Sky Syndicate, present tense.
**Influence on the player:** The devil's bargain hire — prosperity now,
entanglement on a schedule you don't control. Pairs horribly (wonderfully)
with Elias if you ever crew both.

## Design notes

- **Juno Vale** (prologue) is the thirteenth face: she predates this roster and
  is given, not recruited — the fixed point the twelve orbit.
- Named recruits surface in their home-world cantinas alongside procedural
  hires; each can be hired once per game (`char_<key>` flag). Tomas comes only
  through the undercity bond scene.
- Agendas are expressed today through the systems that already run: secrets
  (`union_deserter` is a live patrol landmine; `informant`/`wanted`/`ex_pirate`
  are seeded for hooks), tells, trust-gated reveals, and personal quests.
  **Next pass:** agenda *beats* — periodic scripted moments where the dishonest
  agendas actually bill you (Vex's collectors, Miri's manifest leak, Corbin's
  gossip coming home) and the honest ones pay off (Ada's testimony, Dez's mail
  network warning you of a sweep).
